For FetalyzeAI on **CTU-CHB / CTU-UHB**, do **not** judge the model mainly by accuracy. You need metrics that show whether the model catches dangerous cases, stays calibrated, and avoids overconfident mistakes.

## Must-have evaluation metrics

### 1. Accuracy

Basic overall correctness.

```text
accuracy = correct predictions / total predictions
```

Use it, but do not make it your main claim.

---

### 2. Balanced accuracy

Very important because your classes are imbalanced.

```text
balanced accuracy = average recall across all classes
```

This prevents the model from looking good just because it predicts “low risk” often.

---

### 3. Macro-F1

One of your most important metrics.

```text
macro-F1 = average F1 across low risk, watch closely, high risk
```

This treats all classes equally, including the smaller high-risk class.

---

### 4. Weighted F1

Useful secondary metric.

```text
weighted F1 = F1 weighted by class size
```

This shows overall performance while respecting class distribution.

---

### 5. High-risk recall / sensitivity

This is probably the **most important clinical metric**.

```text
high-risk recall = high-risk cases correctly found / all true high-risk cases
```

For FetalyzeAI, missing a high-risk case is worse than creating an extra warning.

Track this clearly:

```text
High-risk recall
High-risk false-negative count
High-risk false-negative rate
```

---

### 6. Watch-closely recall

Also important because borderline fetal cases matter.

```text
watch recall = watch cases correctly found / all true watch cases
```

The “watch closely” class may be where FetalyzeAI is most useful.

---

### 7. Specificity

Shows how well the model avoids false alarms.

For high-risk detection:

```text
specificity = true low/watch cases correctly not called high-risk
```

You want high sensitivity, but specificity still matters so the tool does not alarm too often.

---

### 8. AUROC

Use AUROC for:

```text
low vs rest
watch vs rest
high-risk vs rest
macro-average AUROC
```

AUROC tells you ranking ability, but it can look too optimistic when classes are imbalanced.

---

### 9. AUPRC

Very important for high-risk cases.

```text
AUPRC = area under precision-recall curve
```

For imbalanced medical data, **high-risk AUPRC** is often more meaningful than AUROC.

Report:

```text
High-risk AUPRC
Macro AUPRC
```

---

### 10. Confusion matrix

Absolutely needed.

Use labels:

```text
Low Risk
Watch Closely
High Risk
```

This lets you see:

```text
high-risk predicted as low risk
watch predicted as low risk
low risk predicted as high risk
```

The worst cell is:

```text
true high-risk → predicted low-risk
```

That is the dangerous miss.

---

## Calibration and uncertainty metrics

These are extremely important for a clinical-style AI tool.

### 11. Brier score

Measures probability quality.

```text
lower Brier score = better calibrated probabilities
```

A model saying 95% confidence and being wrong should be punished.

---

### 12. Expected Calibration Error, ECE

Shows whether confidence matches reality.

Example:

```text
When the model says 80% confident, is it actually right about 80% of the time?
```

Report:

```text
ECE
calibration curve
reliability diagram
```

---

### 13. Negative log loss

Good for probability quality.

```text
log loss punishes confident wrong predictions
```

This is useful when comparing calibrated models.

---

### 14. Uncertainty coverage

Since FetalyzeAI should flag uncertain cases, measure:

```text
What percent of cases are marked uncertain?
How accurate is the model on confident cases?
How many high-risk misses are caught by the uncertainty flag?
```

Track:

```text
uncertain rate
accuracy on confident cases
high-risk recall on confident cases
high-risk cases flagged as uncertain
```

This is very important for your app.

---

### 15. Selective prediction curve

This is advanced but very good.

Ask:

```text
If the model only makes predictions when confidence is high, how accurate is it?
```

Show:

```text
coverage vs accuracy
coverage vs high-risk recall
```

Example:

```text
At 80% coverage, model accuracy = X
At 60% coverage, model accuracy = Y
```

This proves uncertainty is useful.

---

## CTG-specific metrics

These make FetalyzeAI look unique.

### 16. Fetal Reserve Score correlation

If your model outputs fetal reserve score, test whether it correlates with clinical outcomes.

Compare fetal reserve score against:

```text
pH
base deficit
Apgar5
risk label
```

Metrics:

```text
Spearman correlation
Pearson correlation
AUC for low reserve detecting high-risk outcome
```

---

### 17. Deceleration Burden Index validation

Test whether higher deceleration burden is associated with worse outcomes.

Report:

```text
mean DBI by class
DBI correlation with pH
DBI AUC for high-risk prediction
```

Example:

```text
Low Risk DBI: low
Watch Closely DBI: medium
High Risk DBI: high
```

This validates your custom feature.

---

### 18. Contraction Stress Response validation

Measure whether delayed recovery after contractions relates to worse outcomes.

Report:

```text
average recovery time by class
late-response score by class
correlation with pH
AUC for high-risk detection
```

This supports your unique clinical idea.

---

### 19. Signal quality subgroup performance

Split test cases by signal quality:

```text
good signal
acceptable signal
poor signal
```

Report performance for each group.

This tells you whether the model fails when data is noisy.

Metrics:

```text
accuracy by signal quality
high-risk recall by signal quality
uncertainty rate by signal quality
```

A good model should become more uncertain when signal quality is poor.

---

### 20. Timeline deterioration metrics

If you use 5-minute windows, evaluate whether risk increases near delivery.

Track:

```text
final 30-minute risk score
final 10-minute risk score
risk slope over time
maximum risk window
```

Compare those to pH/outcome.

Metrics:

```text
AUC of final-window risk
AUC of max-window risk
correlation between risk slope and pH
```

This makes FetalyzeAI different from a static classifier.

---

## Validation design metrics

### 21. Record-level split performance

Always report:

```text
record-level train/validation/test split
```

Not random window split.

In app stats, show:

```text
Train records:
Validation records:
Test records:
Windows used:
No record leakage: yes
```

---

### 22. Cross-validation mean ± std

Use 5-fold grouped cross-validation.

Report:

```text
balanced accuracy: mean ± std
macro-F1: mean ± std
high-risk recall: mean ± std
ECE: mean ± std
```

This shows stability.

---

### 23. Bootstrap confidence intervals

For your final test set, report 95% confidence intervals.

Example:

```text
High-risk recall: 0.82 [0.68, 0.94]
Macro-F1: 0.71 [0.63, 0.79]
Balanced accuracy: 0.76 [0.69, 0.83]
```

This is much better than single numbers.

---

## Error analysis metrics

### 24. False-negative analysis

Create a table of all dangerous misses.

For every true high-risk case predicted low/watch, show:

```text
record_id
true label
predicted label
confidence
uncertainty
pH
fetal reserve score
deceleration burden
signal quality
```

This is critical for clinical trust.

---

### 25. False-alarm analysis

For low-risk cases predicted high-risk, show:

```text
record_id
confidence
uncertainty
deceleration burden
signal quality
reason for false alarm
```

This helps reduce alert fatigue.
