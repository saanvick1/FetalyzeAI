I could access the live page title — **“FetalyzeAI — CTG Second-Reader”** — but the site content did not expose much readable text through the browser tool. Based on the live site positioning and your FetalyzeAI goals, here are the most important suggestions.

## 1. Change the hero message to sound more clinical and credible

Current direction seems to be:

> FetalyzeAI — CTG Second-Reader

Better hero:

> **FetalyzeAI**
> **Uncertainty-aware CTG second reader for fetal monitoring research**
> Helping clinicians and researchers identify concerning, uncertain, or borderline fetal monitoring patterns from cardiotocography data.

Add a safety line directly under it:

> Research-stage decision-support tool. Not intended for diagnosis, treatment, or replacement of clinician judgment.

This makes the project safer and more professional.

---

## 2. Add a “How It Works” section

Use a simple 4-step flow:

```text
CTG Signal Upload
        ↓
Signal Quality Check
        ↓
FHR + UC Pattern Analysis
        ↓
Risk + Uncertainty + Explanation
```

For the website, say:

> FetalyzeAI analyzes fetal heart rate, uterine contraction patterns, variability, decelerations, signal quality, and uncertainty to support review of CTG tracings.

---

## 3. Add the unique clinical features

The site should not look like a generic ML classifier. Add these as feature cards:

### Fetal Reserve Score

Estimates how well the fetus appears to tolerate labor stress over time.

### Deceleration Burden Index

Measures the severity, recurrence, duration, and recovery pattern of decelerations.

### Contraction Stress Response

Analyzes how fetal heart rate responds after uterine contractions.

### Uncertainty-Aware Output

Flags borderline or low-confidence cases for clinician review instead of forcing overconfident predictions.

### Signal Quality Check

Identifies missing, noisy, or unreliable CTG segments before prediction.

These make FetalyzeAI feel much more original.

## 5. Add a “Datasets Used” section

Use only the strongest three:

### CTGDL — Primary development dataset

Use as the main multi-source CTG waveform dataset. Mention that the pipeline verifies real CTGDL files and does not use synthetic placeholder data.

### CTU-UHB / CTU-CHB — Clinical benchmark

Used for fetal heart rate, uterine contraction signals, and outcome-based evaluation.

### UCI / Kaggle Fetal Health — Tabular benchmark

Used only as a feature-based comparison benchmark, not as clinical proof.

Website wording:

> FetalyzeAI is designed to avoid dataset-specific memorization by training and testing across waveform and tabular CTG datasets.
